# Reference audit

The current draft has a solid core bibliography for the autoconvolution bound, but a few parts of the prose would be better supported with additional primary sources.

Directly relevant sources to keep in the bibliography:

- `CS17` for the finite reduction and the original Sidon-set connection.
- `MV10` for the earlier best lower bound and the sharpness discussion around autoconvolutions.
- `MO2004`, `MO2006`, and `MO2009` for the continuous/discrete Sidon-set context.
- `CillerueloVinuesa2008` for the Schinzel-Schmidt connection.
- `SS2002` for the classical upper bound in the Tao constant page.
- `Bomze2014` for the regular-grid / simplex-rounding step.
- `Tao` for the live constant page and historical bound table.

Claims that should be explicitly cited or rewritten if the manuscript is cleaned up further:

- The Sidon-set motivation in the introduction around lines 164--171 should cite `MO2004`, `MO2006`, or `MO2009`, not only `CS17`.
- The discretization discussion around lines 223--360 would read more credibly with `Bomze2014` as the simplex-grid reference.
- The known-bounds table around lines 175--183 should cite `SS2002`, `MV10`, `CS17`, and the current `Tao` page.

Two entries in the hand-written bibliography are real papers, but they are not the right support for the claims currently made in this draft:

- `White22` is actually Ethan Patrick White, *A New Bound for Erdos' Minimum Overlap Problem* (Acta Arith. 208 (2023), 235--255, doi:10.4064/aa220728-7-6). It is related background, but it does not support the `C_{1a}` bound as written.
- `BL25` is actually Boyer and Li, *An Improved Example for an Autoconvolution Inequality* (Experimental Mathematics, published online 15 Feb 2026, doi:10.1080/10586458.2025.2607423). It is tangential unless the manuscript is expanded to discuss that separate inequality.

If the manuscript is converted from the embedded bibliography to BibTeX, the safest move is to keep the verified entries above and drop or relabel the two unrelated placeholders unless the prose is updated to use them.
